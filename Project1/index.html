What Small Businesses Don't Get about Big Data

Mar. 17, 2017

Big Data is a phrase used to mean a massive volume of both structured and unstructured data that is so large it is difficult to process using traditional database and software techniques. In most enterprise scenarios the volume of data is too big or it moves too fast or it exceeds current processing capacity. Most small businesses think human touch outweighs anything automated but tends to potentially inhibit success at the expense of data collection. Others believe that revenue will not be enhanced due to data. Using data can increase profit and saves time in the long run. Then you have those that believe data should immediately solve problems. Slow down and recoup! With the use of data, small businesses can make stronger decisions on future business strategies. Data being too broad and collecting data can become expensive, serve as further misconceptions of small businesses. But, those worries are much too little compared to what can be gained from collecting data.
